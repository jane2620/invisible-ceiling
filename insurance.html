The Invisible Ceiling

Insurance Agent

Licenses to be an insurance sales agent are given by an independent Georgia licensing board. Many of the requirements are dependent on the type of insurance you would like to sell.
Attribute Value
Fee $200.0
Steps
  • Complete all pre-licensing education
  • Make a reservation for an exam and pay exam fee
  • Take exam
  • Complete citizenship affidavit
  • Apply for a license (and meet application requirements, description below)
  • Complete fingerprint registration
  • Upload documents to online portals
Criminal Record Impact Applicants are required to "be a good character" meaning that licenses can be denied if the applicant has been convicted of a felony or crime involving moral turpitude. Any felony, misdemeanor, or disciplinary action must be submitted even if it has been pardoned, expunged, dismissed, or deferred, and even if you have been told it will not appear on your criminal record.

Licenses can also be denied if they've been arrested and sentenced for certain crimes, including cases with first offender treatment. They can also be denied a license if they fail to report these criminal prosecutions to the Commissioner, so it's important to report all criminal history. If a license is denied, the applicant can file a written petition to dispute the denial of a license, and the board will describe why your license was denied.
