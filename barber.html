The Invisible Ceiling

Barber

Apprenticeship must be completed through 36 months' of training to gain 3,000 hours of experience. The apprentice must pass a licensing exam at the end of the apprenticeship.
Attribute Value
Fee $90.0
Steps
  • Complete, sign, and notarize application and fee
  • Verify Salon/Shop license
  • Verify Supervisor license
  • Disclose disciplinary action/sanctions through court documents, letters of explanation, and/or enrollment in alternative to discipline
  • Get secure & verifiable document
  • Get affidavit of citizenship
  • Get immigration documents (if applicable)
Criminal Record Impact Application may be denied if judge deems criminal record is relevant to barbering, or if disciplinary activity isn't fully reported. Any felony, misdemeanor, or disciplinary action must be submitted even if it has been pardoned, expunged, dismissed, or deferred, and even if you have been told it will not appear on your criminal record.

Georgia Secretary of State licensing boards can deny licenses for individuals who have been arrested, charged, and sentences for any felony or crime involving "moral turpitude" where first offender treatment without adjudication of guilt was granted or an adjudication of guilt was not entered, other than a plea of nolo contendere. Licenses can also be denied if criminal records are not fully and accurately reported. Applicants can appear in front of licensing board if they want to present their case, including qualifications and circumstances. If an application is denied, the applicant can petition against the licensing board. Please reach out to the Georgia Justice Project for more information.
